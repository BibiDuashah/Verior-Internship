Pakistan Clinches Thrilling Victory Against India in T20 World Cup

By Sports Correspondent | Melbourne Published: 2 hours ago

Pakistan players celebrate after defeating India in their T20 World Cup opener at MCG. (Photo: Unsplash)

In a nail-biting finish at the Melbourne Cricket Ground, Pakistan defeated arch-rivals India by 5 wickets in their opening match of the T20 World Cup. Chasing a target of 160, Pakistan reached the target with one ball to spare, thanks to a brilliant unbeaten 50 by Mohammad Rizwan.

The match lived up to its billing as one of the most anticipated clashes of the tournament, with both teams displaying exceptional skills. India posted 159/8 in their 20 overs, with Virat Kohli top-scoring with 82 runs. Pakistan's bowling attack, led by Shaheen Afridi (3/28) and Haris Rauf (2/36), kept the Indian batsmen in check for most of the innings.

Match Highlights

The Pakistani chase got off to a rocky start with captain Babar Azam falling for a golden duck to Arshdeep Singh. However, Mohammad Rizwan steadied the innings with a composed 50 off 42 balls, while Shan Masood contributed a valuable 35. The match went down to the wire with Pakistan needing 13 off the last over.

Post-match, captain Babar Azam praised his team's fighting spirit: "This is why we play cricket - for moments like these. The way the boys fought back after early setbacks shows the character of this team. Rizwan was exceptional under pressure."

Turning Points

The match had several key moments:

  • Shaheen Afridi's opening spell where he dismissed Rohit Sharma and KL Rahul
  • Virat Kohli's counter-attacking innings that took India to a competitive total
  • Mohammad Nawaz's crucial 14-run cameo in the death overs
  • Mohammad Rizwan's calmness under pressure during the chase

The victory puts Pakistan in a strong position in Group 2, with their next match against South Africa on Wednesday. India will look to bounce back against Netherlands on Thursday.
